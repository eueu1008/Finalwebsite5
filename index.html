Green Coca-Cola Bottles (1962)
Material: Oil on Canvas
Dimensions: 209.55 x 144.78 cm
Locations: Whitney Museum of American Art, New York, USA

Andy Warhol made art available to the everyday man and everybody understood it.
Coca-Cola paintings represented democratic equality. This painting is composed of
112 empty green glass bottles of Coca Cola, lined up into 7 rows of 16 set on a white
background. At the very bottom of the canvas, there is the coca cola logo perfectly
centred. The space in Warholâ€™s piece is mostly taken up by the empty green glass
bottles but the negative spaces allow the eye to travel through the work. This work
consists of repetition of the bottles. The eyes usually follow the rows from left to right
and from top to bottom. The order and structure of this piece displays balance and
there is a high visual contrast between the white background and the cleanly traced
green of the empty iconic glass bottle. The intense red logo also stands out. To
further this analysis, one can associate the dark green colour with money and the
bright red Coca Cola logo as a symbol of power. This artwork gave a stronger
imagery of an advertisement, which represents the Pop Art movement.
